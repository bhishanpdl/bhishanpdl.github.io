Resampling methods

In statistics there are various ways to get the sample data from the population. The students t-distribution and chi-square distribution are good apporximations for sufficiently large and/or normally-distributed samples. However, when the data is of unknown distribution or sample size is samll resampling tests are required. For example, we can use resamling to get the estimates for sampling distribution of number of connections in a neural net. Here in this blog, I have explored following methods:

  • Sampling with and without replacement
  • Bootstrap (using sampling with replacement)
  • Jackknife (using subsets)
  • Cross validation and LOOCV (using subsets)
  • Permutation resampling (switching labels)
(image src: https://www.slideshare.net/hafidztio/resampling-methods)



Bootstrapping

Bootstrapping is one of the sampling methods to draw out elements from the population. In bootstrapping method we draw items with replacement.
